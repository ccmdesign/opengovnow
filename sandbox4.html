Open Governments are built on two things: information and collaboration. A government that is open releases information about budgeting, contracting, spending, and aid to be freely open and available to the public, in a way that average citizens can understand. Equally important: an Open Government is one that actively invites citizens to be participants in government decision-making. This two-way relationship between citizens and governments is the foundation of Open Government.
